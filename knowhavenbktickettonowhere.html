Ticket to Nowhere

WANTEN, 2025

The cold air bites into my skin, sending pricks up the back of my neck. I feel the odd tense in my legs, my back. The not quite pins-and-needles, yet. I hold my ticket, I stand rapt next to the bus stop sign. An unfocused breath away from a shiver.

This road I am in, it is either mine, or someone's. If it is not mine, then I am lost in the cold, at the mercy of the darkness, of the unseen someone. If it is mine, then the road, rain-wet and winding, span of multiple cities, is mine to own; mine to traverse; mine to make part of my world, of dark corners and overwhelming streets, of accidents and of hearses.
